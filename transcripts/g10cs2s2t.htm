POLICE SAW OF HASHISH
Men fled, threw Drugs into dock

THE midnight import of "hashish" - Indian hemp" - from a ship in Swansea Docks, by two Jamaicans who travelled by car from Liverpool to collect it, was silently watched by police and Customs officers, Swansea magistrates were told yesterday. Then, as the officers closed in, the men threw the packets, containing 4 ½ lbs. of the drug, into King's Dock, and ran in opposite directions.

The Jamaicans, 30-year-old crane driver Roy Thompson, of old Normanby street, and 38-year old motor mechanic Herman Sylvester McKay, of Jermyn street, both of Liverpool, were sent to prison for six months on each of three charges concerning the hemp, the sentences to run concurrently.
