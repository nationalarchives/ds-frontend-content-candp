"The number of convicts in
Dartmoor and Portland placed upon extra
food in consequence of loss of weight
during the 3 years ending 30th June, 1895
was 1162. This number is exclusive of those
admitted into Hospital for loss weight
and debility.
In the discussion of this subject
it must be remembered that serious loss
of weight may be the first step towards
the setting up of organic disease. This
is an important consideration which must
not be lost sight of. It is not capable
of direct proof, but the extent of organic
disease in Convict Prisons of late years
has caused me some anxiety.

(Sgd) R.M. Gover
