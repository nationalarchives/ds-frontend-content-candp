Case Study 2 - Public attitudes to the police
 

 

Carry out your own survey of attitudes to the police. A simple way of doing this is to set up a number of statements. People have only to agree or disagree with each one.
Here are a few statements to get you started:

1. The police think that all young people are out to cause trouble.
2. If I was in trouble I would go to the police.
3. In the past the police knew how to handle young people.
4. The police are only doing their job.
5. The police make arrests so as to get promotion.
6. You should always try to help the police.

Add three or four statements of your own.
All you have to do is add up the responses: someone who responds "Agree" to numbers 1, 3 & 5 and "disagree" to numbers 2, 4 and 6 is hostile to the police, and so on.
You would find it interesting to survey people from different age, class, gender and ethnic groups and keep the results separate in order to compare them.
