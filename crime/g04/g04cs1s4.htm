SP 45/10; extract from a Proclamation issued by Charles I, 1630. It recognises the distinction made in previous Acts that some people are too poor to work and should be looked after, while others are vagabonds and should be punished. It also links vagabonds with "pestilence" (= the plague).
 
  As you read these four sources, look for:
1. What seems to be the government attitude to these poor people?
2. What is the government doing to them?
In Source 4, This is not a new law: which old laws are referred to? What is Charles saying about them?
