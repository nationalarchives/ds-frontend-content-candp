Britain at one time could be said to be a community
policing itself. The policeman was very much a
part of the community, and known to the citizens
in his area of operation. With the advent of modern
police methods and an increasing reliance upon elec-
tronic and technological means, the ordinary police-
man is being placed at a distance from his community.
This leads to the undesirable situation emphasised
in particular sections of the community as "them"
and "us". In simple terms there is a reduction of
trust.
