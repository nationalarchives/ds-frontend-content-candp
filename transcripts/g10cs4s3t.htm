NOTE FOR LOBBY CORRESPONDANTS

CRIMINAL STATISTICS, 1957.
(Cmnd. 529. price 8 shillings net)

The rising trend in the total number of indictable offences known to the
police that began in 1955 and continued during 1956 continued steeply
throughout 1957. The total for 1957 was 4% higher than at the previous peak
in 1951 and 14% higher than in 1945. Judging by the statistics of convictions,
the increase in crime was much more rapid among juveniles over 14 and young
men and women than among younger children or older men and women. The most
serious proportionate increases were in robbery and in breaking and entering
offences, and the increase in offences of violence against the person was
also large.
