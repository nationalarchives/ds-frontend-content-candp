(i)
Case History
An 11 year old boy
complained that he had been
assaulted by a police officer during
an interview at a primary school.
The officer admitted slapping
the child but claimed to have been
provoked by his abusive remarks.
The force proposed that the
officer be formally admonished.
However, the Authority decided to
recommend a disciplinary charge
of abuse of authority. This was
accepted and the officer pleaded
guilty. He was fined £500.


(ii)
Case History
Mr S made a complaint and a
civil claim against police for
unlawful arrest and assault.
Following an anonymous
telephone call alleging that he
had a gun in his car, Mr S had
been arrested by armed officers.
He resisted and had to be
restrained. He claimed that his
wrist had been broken and arrived
to make his complaint with his
arm in plaster.
During the investigation
further anonymous telephone
calls were received and it became
clear that these had been made by
Mr S himself. It was also
confirmed that Mr. S had not
suffered any injury but had tricked
a nurse into applying the plaster
cast to his wrist.
Mr S admitted to engineering
the whole incident in an attempt
to obtain substantial damages. He
was charged with deception and
wasting police time. He apologised and formally withdrew his complaint.
