II. The causes of the increase in misdoing may be roughly
divided under three heads:-
(a) The War Spirit, which fosters a desire for lawless
adventure, and appears to give a legitimate excuse for
the exercise of tendencies previously held to be repre-
hensible.
The constant talk of raids, reprisals, and bomb
throwing, of killing and wounding, and the sight of
bayonet practice.

(next page)

(b) War conditions such as the absence of the fathers
at the Front; the increased employment away from home
of the mothers; the shortened school-hours and serious
lack of men teachers; the darkened streets; the
lessened number of police, and the absence of social
workers from boys' clubs, church brigades, and "scout"
organizations.
(c) Ill-advised Cinema Shows, in which clever dodging
of police and penalties is shown, and criminal actions
made to seem adventurous and romantic.
