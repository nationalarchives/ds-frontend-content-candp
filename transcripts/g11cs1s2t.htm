The Patrols, however, must be careful not to behave
as if they deemed all drivers offenders at heart, ready to
indulge in reckless or dangerous conduct whenever opportunity
offers. Motorists as a whole are law abiding. It is only
a minority of drivers who are selfish, inconsiderate and
dangerous users of the road. The great majority are most
anxious to do the right thing, thus ranging themselves (so to
speak) on the side of the Police. The Patrols must therefore
feel that they are co-operating with the public, and they must
be careful not to estrange nor to lose the sympathy of the
decent motorist.
