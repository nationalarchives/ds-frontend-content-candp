Our Ref DDA 71/3/6

Home Office,
Whitehall, S.W.1

6th April, 1955

Dear Turnell,

The very volume of the
seizures indicates that in the ports where the problem has been
worst for some time, Customs officers are displaying commendable
zeal and ingenuity in their rummaging, and I am glad to have
this opportunity of recording our appreciation to their efforts.
But it does occur to us that there may be ports where this
particular problem has not previously been met and to which
traffic may now be diverted simply because Customs and police
between them have made the more usual ports of entry too
difficult and dangerous.

(Text Omitted)

I expect your people already know all about it, but for
what it is worth I would mention that any ships coming from
Rangoon must be regarded as suspect, and so to a lesser extent
must any tanker coming from the Persian Gulf.
