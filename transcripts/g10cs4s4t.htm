More gun-crime
claims Sandys

MR. DUNCUN SANDYS BY
yesterday predicted Michael Steemson
that last year's crime
figures would show that
crimes involving firearms
have trebled in the three
years since hanging for
murder was abolished.
Mr. Sandys, Tory M.P. for
Streatham, and former
Cabinet Minister, said :
"Since the suspension of
capital punishment, criminals
have shown a greater readiness
to carry guns."
He said that in 1964, the last
year before the death penalty
was suspended, there were 731
offences involving firearms in
England and Wales.
