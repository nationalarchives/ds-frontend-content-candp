(HO 144/514)

Daily Chronicle 18th October, 1987

The death of William Cooper in Norwich
prison will not be without some good result
if it calls public attention to the fact that
the treadmill, that relic of wicked and use-
less torture, survives in our prison system.
The doctor passes the man for the tread-
mill, gives him medicine and sends him back
to the mill, when he complains of illness.
He admits at the inquest that the man's
exertions caused his death, and that he would
not have sent him to the mill at all had he
known the facts. The governor finds the
man lying on his cell floor, and refuses to
believe that anything but a sulking fit is
the matter. And so he is left to die
Not the men bit the system is at fault.
But what a system !
