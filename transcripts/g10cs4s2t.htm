(i) Daily Mail 26.8.54

Half British 'crime'
is by motorists
TEDDY BOYS ARE BETTER
By a Daily Mail Reporter
A HOME OFFICE report on law-breaking in Britain
yesterday showed that the class responsible for
more offences against the law in 1953 then any other
was - the motoring class.
This "criminal" class, in-
cluding motor-cyclists, cyclists,
drivers of cars, lorries, and
other "mechanically-propelled
vehicles," was responsible for
more than half of all offences
committed throughout Eng-
land and Wales.

(ii) Daily Mirror 27.8.54

Daily Mirror
KEEP IT UP,
KIDS!
HURRAH for statistics! At last they
prove something we want to hear
about.
The crime figures are falling.
They're falling generally. But most
important, the number of serious crimes
committed by CHILDREN is lower than
at any time since 1947.
That's one in the eye for the Blimps
and Dammits who rumble about modern
youth going to the dogs.
Let's roll the figures round our tongues
for a minute. In 1951, 28,000 children
between eight and fourteen were found
guilty of indictable offences - larceny,
breaking and entering, that sort of thing.
In 1952 there were 26,000. Last year the
figure was down to 22,000.
This Happy Breed
See how they drop! Now we can start
waving good-bye to the "crime wave."
