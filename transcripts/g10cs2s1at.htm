WHEN people speak of smuggling nowadays they think only of stray travellers to and from the Continent who try to evade payment of duty on bottles of perfume or small quantities of spirit. They regard the days of contraband cargo running under cover of darkness as a thing of the very distant past, and they talk as though the smuggling instinct had been lost by those who live on our coasts. But we know better.

Nightly we learn of strange boats putting at quiet places along the coast,but by the time the spot is reached the craft have vanished, and the ghostly

figures who stole ashore from them have passed into the darkness out of our ken, or the accomplices of the fishermen we find with their boats have been equally quick in vanishing. There is no difficulty about obtaining along the coast liberal quantities of spirits and wines that have not paid duty, or that have never been in bond on this side. Those who know their way about can always lay hands on supplies of undiluted spirits and other articles.
