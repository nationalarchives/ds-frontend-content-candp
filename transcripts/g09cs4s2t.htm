(HO 73/3)

It is a strange but singular fact, that young boys are more hardened in
crime, more daring in exploit, more reckless of punishment, and more trouble-
some in prison, than adults. These boys appear to have ventured all their
energies upon crime, as a gambler would a stake. They are hailed with joy
among their companions on their release from gaol; they hasten to put into
practice the lessons they may have learnt from fellow-prisoners; for a prison
is a thief's college.
