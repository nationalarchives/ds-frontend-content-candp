To Chief Constable., C.l.Dept.

With reference to the methods adopted by criminals
for the disposal of stolen motor cars.
In the case of Harrington and others just concluded,
a large number of stolen motor cars had been successfully re-
registered and sold in the ordinary way of business.
Two methods were used by these persons; one was to
buy modern motor cars which had been involved in an accident and
were badly damaged. These cars, together with Registration
book, were purchased from insurance companies, at a very low
figure, as salvage. The cars were usually beyond repair, or
in such a state that repair could not be done on a profitable basis.
The salvage car was removed to a yard and broken up,
but the Registration Book was carefully preserved until a car of
similar make, and in every way identical with the smash car, could
be stolen. Having stolen a suitable car, the original numbers
were removed, and those from the Registration Book of the salvage
car, were substituted.
A Road Fund licence would then be obtained from the
Licensing authorities who would be under the impression that it
was to be applied to the car purchased from the Insurance
Company.
