Finger-prints.
The past year was one of special interest in the history of the Finger-print
Branch, for the month of July 1951 marked the 50th anniversary of the
adoption of the finger-print system devised by Sir Edward Henry, Bt.,

(text omitted)

Beginning as a humble collection of finer-prints recorded haphazard on
anthropometric cards and housed in a simple chest of drawers, the collection
of classified finger-prints now requires 56 cabinets, containing 3,487 pigeon-
holes, to house its 1,190,223 records. Many yards of shelving are also required
for storing duplicate forms of re-convicted persons.
